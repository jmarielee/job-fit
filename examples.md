# Examples — Three Worked Decisions

Each example shows the same pipeline: labels in → math → routed call out. Example 1 is the live demo's actual data; 2 and 3 are edge cases the obvious rules don't cover.

---

## Example 1 — Viable but Exposed (the demo case)

**Pairing:** Mid-level backend engineer (Python-heavy, "Software Engineer II") vs. a senior backend role at a post-layoff Series B (Go/Rust shop).

**Key labels:**

| JD item | tier | centrality | status |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5+ years backend systems | required | core | meets |
| Senior-level end-to-end ownership | required | core | **partial** |
| Distributed systems / pipelines | required | core | meets |
| Independent decisions at scale | required | supporting | **missing** |
| Go/Rust production fluency | preferred | supporting | missing |
| Public portfolio / OSS | preferred | peripheral | missing |
| Technical writing | preferred | peripheral | missing |

**Math:** Only **one** required core/supporting item is fully missing → the 45-point cap does **not** fire (it needs 2+). Required partials and preferred misses pull the base to 62.2; the best mapped strength (supporting, +6) is headroom-scaled to +2.3. **Score: 64.**

**Committee:** Recruiter 68 (advance, nervously), HM 58 (fence — wants "I owned it," hears "I contributed"), internal peer 43 (would cut — metrics without mechanism read as rehearsed). Split: 2 lean apply, 1 leans skip.

**Routed call:** **Apply with Caution** — score clears 55, but strengths don't dominate gaps (S ≯ 1.3G), so the unqualified "Apply" is withheld. The fix-list leads with rewriting the top 3 bullets to ownership language, and the report closes with the referral-ask DM already drafted — 72 words, one credibility hook, one 15-minute ask, ready to send.

## Example 2 — Edge case: the "or eligibility to obtain" license

**Pairing:** Career-changer vs. an insurance role requiring "Property & Casualty license, *or eligibility to obtain within 90 days* (training provided)."

**The trap a naive screener falls into:** label the license `required / core / missing` → the candidate looks gated out of a role explicitly designed to train them.

**What the rules do instead:** the JD itself offers the credential as acquirable → `obtainable: true`. A missing-but-obtainable item is scored as `partial` (0.5 gap, not 1.0), and it can **never** trigger the required-gate cap. The act of obtaining the license routes to **priority actions** ("confirm eligibility, note it in your cover line"), not to the deficit column.

**Second-order rule firing here:** the JD's "training provided" language marks it entry-level-friendly, so the benchmark candidate is calibrated to a *fellow career-changer* — not a licensed 10-year veteran the role never asked for. **Routed call: Apply**, with the license eligibility named as the first move.

## Example 3 — Edge case: paper-clear but the room says no (divergence alert)

**Pairing:** Candidate meets every listed requirement of a staff-level role — years, stack, domain — but every claim is unverifiable: no public work, no ownership language, generic bullets.

**The math says:** all required items `meets` → high base score, ~78 after the realism ceiling's gravity.

**The committee says:** HM 52 and internal peer 44 both lean skip — not because of eligibility, but because of *evidence*. 1 apply, 2 skip.

**What the operator does:** the verdict stays score-driven (**Strong Candidate** — the committee is nuance, not a veto), but the recommendation drops to **Apply with Caution** because the apply-vote and edge-dominance conditions fail. And the observer is *required* to name the divergence:

> "On paper you clear the bar cleanly — the room's hesitation isn't eligibility, it's proof. One public artifact and one 'I owned X end-to-end' claim would flip both skeptics."

This is the case where most fit-checkers fail in the opposite direction of Example 2: they see green checkmarks and say "great fit!" The operator surfaces the gap between *qualified* and *believable* — and tells the user it's the believability that needs work.

---

## What the three examples demonstrate together

1. The operator **decides every time** — three different routed calls, zero "it depends, you tell me."
2. **Edge cases are encoded, not hand-waved** — obtainability, level calibration, and divergence each have explicit rules with explicit triggers.
3. The output is **trustworthy enough to act on** because the number is reproducible math and every objection cites its evidence.
